to accept—akzeptieren, annehmen, entgegennehmen, in Kauf nehmen, hinnehmen

The usual word is annehmen which can take both a concrete object like payment or a job and a less tangible object such as help or an invitation, but can also be used without an object, e.g.

Example 1. Beispielen

Ich habe (die Stelle/das Angebot) angenommen.

I accepted (the job/offer).


Akzeptieren is a somewhat elevated synonym of annehmen , e.g.

Example 2. Beispielen

Das Judentum akzeptiert die Homosexualität.

Judaism accepts homosexuality.


In more elevated language where "to accept" is synonymous with "to receive" entgegennehmen will be encountered, but it is often interchangeable with annehmen , e.g.

Example 3. Beispielen

Er nahm Schmiergelder in Millionenhöhe entgegen.

He accepted (=received) bribes in the millions

Der Wissenschaftler hat den Preis im Namen des kubanischen Volkes entgegengenommen.

The scientist accepted the prize in the name of the Cuban people.


Where the object of "to accept" is something to your detriment or dissatisfaction, and thus you accept the inevitable only with resignation or reluctance, hinnehmen is required, e.g.

Example 4. Beispielen

Die Partei wird diese Niederlage einfach hinnehmen müssen.

The party will simply have to accept this defeat.


In Kauf nehmen is similar in meaning to hinnehmen but implies somewhat less suffering or inevitability, e.g.

Example 5. Beispielen

Wegen der Protestaktionen haben Fahrer viele Staus auf den Autobahnen in Kauf nehmen müssen.

Drivers had to accept (contend with) many traffic jams on the freeways as a result of the demonstrations.
